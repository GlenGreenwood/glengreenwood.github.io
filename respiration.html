Respiration

Cellular Respiration:

Cellular respiration is the process by which energy stored in glucose is converted into usable cellular energy.

  1. Glycolysis

    2. Takes place in the cytoplasm.

    1. Glucose is split into two pyruvates ["pirates"]
    2. An enzyme is used to remove one phosphate from the result to reconstruct ATP
    3. 2 ATP are used, and 4 ATP are produced (net 2)
    4. NAD+ picks up hydrogen

  2. TCA Cycle (Krebs Cycle)

    3. Takes place in the mitochondria.

    1. Hydrogen flows through ATP Synthase
    2. ATP is produced from ADP and Pi (photophosphorylation/falalalala)
    3. NADP⁺ picks up hydrogen to form NADPH

  3. ETS (Electron transport system)

    4. Takes place in the Mitochondria

    1. NADH and FADH₂ donate electrons
    2. The electron's negetivity move the H+ to the intermembrane space?
    3. This creates a hydrogen/energy gradient.

  4. Oxidative Phosphorylation

    5. Takes place in the Mitochondria

    1. Hydrogen flows through ATP Synthase, thereby turning it.
    2. ADP and Pi are combined into ATP
    3. Oxygen combines with Hydrogen and becomes water

A Word about Falalalala (Phosphorilation)

There are two main types of phosphorilation. The first is known as substrate-level phosphorilation. This variety uses an enzyme within the cytoplasm OR mitochondria to form ATP from ADP and a phosphate attached to a "high-energy substrate" (such as a pyruvate).

Oxidative phosphorilation is the process by which most ATP is produced. It uses hydrogen-powered ATP Synthase to combine free ADP and Pi. As the hydrogen fills the matrix of the mitochondra it combines with free oxygen and becomes water.

More Details:

  • Glucose is one of three basic sugars (Carbohydrates, monosaccarides) the others are fructose and galactose.
  • Oxygen can speed up and increase the efficiency of respriation. It is necessary to the process.
  • Oxygen prevents the matrix and synthase from clogging
  • Fermentation is an alternate method of energy production that doesn't need oxygen, but it's products are typically somewhat toxic.
